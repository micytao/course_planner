# UC Open Day Course Planner — Student User Guide

Welcome to the UC Course Planner! This tool helps you explore degrees, choose a major, and build a personalised Year 1 course plan at the University of Canterbury.

---

## Getting Started

1. Open your web browser and go to the Course Planner link provided at the Open Day.
2. You will land on the **Home** page, which shows three options:
   - **Chat Advisor** — talk to a virtual course advisor
   - **Course Wizard** — follow a step-by-step guide
   - **Sample Scenarios** — see example student journeys

Pick whichever mode suits you best. You can switch between them at any time using the **sidebar** on the left, where pages are grouped under **Planning** and **Knowledge** sections.

---

## Option A: Course Wizard (Recommended for First-Time Users)

The wizard walks you through seven simple steps to build your course plan. Just click buttons and fill in a few details — no typing required.

### Step 1 — Study Level

Choose **Undergraduate (first degree)**. The tool currently focuses on undergraduate study. If you are interested in postgraduate study, contact the relevant faculty directly.

### Step 2 — What Interests You?

Browse seven interest areas that map to UC's faculties:

| If you're interested in... | You'll explore... |
|---|---|
| People, Culture, Languages, History | Faculty of Arts |
| Business, Finance, Management | UC Business School |
| Teaching, Working with Young People | Faculty of Education |
| Building Things, Technology, Engineering | Faculty of Engineering |
| Health, Wellbeing, Sport, Social Work | Faculty of Health |
| Law, Justice, Criminal Justice | Faculty of Law |
| Science, Environment, Data, Psychology | Faculty of Science |

Click the one that appeals most. Don't worry — you can always go back.

### Step 3 — Choose a Degree

You'll see all the degrees available in your chosen faculty. Each card shows:

- **Degree code and name** (e.g., BCom — Bachelor of Commerce)
- **Total points** and how many years it typically takes
- **Pathway options** (e.g., "1 major + 1 minor" or "fixed programme")
- A **Special entry** warning if the degree has limited places or requires a separate application

Click **Select** on the degree that interests you.

### Step 4 — Choose Your Major

Depending on your degree, you'll choose a:
- **Major** (most degrees) — your area of specialisation
- **Discipline** (Engineering) — your specific branch of engineering
- **Endorsement** (Teaching) — Early Childhood, Primary, or Matauranga Maori
- **Group 1 minor** (BPsycSc) — a psychology specialisation

Some degrees like LLB (Law) and BCJ (Criminal Justice) are fixed programmes with no major to choose — you'll simply continue to the next step.

For BA students: you'll also see an option to choose a **Specialisation** instead of a major + minor.

### Step 5 — Your Background

Fill in a short form about your qualifications:

- **Qualification type** — NCEA, Cambridge, IB, overseas, or mature student
- **University Entrance** — tick if you have (or expect to have) UE
- **Year 13 subjects** — tick Maths, Physics, and/or Chemistry if you studied them at Level 3
- **Domestic student** — tick if you are a New Zealand or Australian citizen/resident

The tool will check whether you meet the entry requirements for your chosen degree and flag anything you need to know (e.g., Engineering recommends Year 13 Maths and Physics; BTchLn requires a special application).

Click **Check & Continue** to proceed.

### Step 6 — Your Year 1 Course Plan

The tool generates a personalised Year 1 plan showing:

- **Semester 1 courses** on the left
- **Semester 2 courses** on the right
- Each course card shows the course code, title, points, and why it's included (core, major, elective, or bridge course)
- A **Total Points** count at the bottom

Review the plan and click **Continue to Summary** when you're happy.

### Step 7 — Summary & Export

This page brings everything together:

- Your **faculty, degree, and major** at the top
- A **table of all Year 1 courses** with codes, titles, semesters, and points
- Any **admission notes** (warnings about special applications, bridge courses, etc.)
- A **Download Plan as Text** button to save your plan and bring it home

You can click **Start Over** to go through the wizard again with different choices.

## Option B: Chat Advisor

If you prefer a conversational approach, the Chat Advisor lets you ask questions in your own words.

### Without AI (default)

When you first open the Chat Advisor, you'll see six **suggestion buttons** to get started:

- "What faculties does UC have?"
- "What degrees are available in Engineering?"
- "Tell me about the Bachelor of Commerce"
- "What majors can I do in a BSc?"
- "Tell me about LAWS101"
- "What's new in 2026?"

Click any button, or type your own question in the text box at the bottom. The tool will respond with information pulled directly from the UC 2026 Calendar.

**Things you can ask about:**

- **Faculties** — "What faculties does UC have?"
- **Degrees** — "What degrees are in Health?" or "Tell me about the BA"
- **Majors** — "What majors can I do in BCom?"
- **Courses** — Type any course code (e.g., "COSC131" or "LAWS101") to see its title, points, prerequisites, and semesters
- **Degree details** — Ask about any degree code like "BSc", "BE(Hons)", or "BHlth"

### With AI enabled

For a richer, more natural conversation:

1. Open the **sidebar** (click the arrow at the top left if it's hidden)
2. Under **AI Configuration**, enter your **API Key** and optionally a **Base URL**
3. Select or enter a **Model** name
4. Click **Connect**

Once connected, you can have a free-flowing conversation. The AI advisor knows all the UC Calendar rules and can:

- Suggest degrees based on your interests
- Explain the differences between similar subjects across faculties (e.g., Psychology in Arts vs Health vs Science)
- Walk you through admission requirements
- Generate a Year 1 course plan
- Answer follow-up questions

### Clearing the chat

Click the **Clear Chat** button in the sidebar to start a fresh conversation.

## Tips for Getting the Most Out of the Tool

- **Not sure which faculty?** Start with the wizard — the interest area descriptions help you map your school subjects and career ideas to the right faculty.
- **Interested in a subject that spans faculties?** Psychology, Economics, Geography, Computer Science, and Statistics are available in more than one faculty. The Chat Advisor can explain the differences, or the wizard will note cross-faculty options.
- **Considering a double degree?** LLB (Law) is almost always taken as a double degree with BA, BCom, or BSc. Engineering offers seven conjoint options. The tool will flag these options where relevant.
- **Missing a prerequisite?** Don't worry — UC offers bridge courses (e.g., EMTH117 for Maths, PHYS111 for Physics) so you can catch up in your first year.
- **Want to take your plan home?** Use the **Download Plan as Text** button on the wizard's summary page (Step 7).

---

## Important Notes

- This tool covers the **UC 2026 Calendar**. Course availability and regulations may change — always confirm with UC directly.
- The course plans are **recommendations**, not official enrolment. You'll finalise your enrolment through UC's official systems.
- For programmes with **special entry** (BFA, BTchLn, BSW, LAWS 200-level), separate applications are required — check deadlines carefully.
- If you have questions the tool can't answer, visit the UC information stands at Open Day or go to [canterbury.ac.nz](https://www.canterbury.ac.nz).

## Need Help?

- **At Open Day:** Ask any UC staff member for assistance using the tool.
- **Online:** Visit [canterbury.ac.nz](https://www.canterbury.ac.nz) or contact the UC Liaison team.
- **Technical issues:** Refresh the page in your browser. If the problem persists, let a staff member know.
